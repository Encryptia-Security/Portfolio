What is VAPT?

Vulnerability Assessment and Penetration Testing (VAPT) is a comprehensive security testing service that combines two distinct but complementary approaches to security testing:

Vulnerability Assessment

The process of identifying, quantifying, and prioritizing vulnerabilities in a system. This is typically an automated process that scans for known vulnerabilities.

Penetration Testing

A simulated cyber attack against your system to check for exploitable vulnerabilities. This is typically a manual process performed by security experts.

The Importance of Vulnerability Assessment and Penetration Testing

In today's digital landscape, organizations face an ever-increasing number of cyber threats. VAPT provides critical insights into your security posture and helps:

Identify security weaknesses before attackers do
Meet compliance requirements (ISO 27001, PCI DSS, HIPAA)
Protect customer data and maintain trust
Avoid financial losses from data breaches
Improve security awareness across the organization
Enhance your security posture and resilience

VAPT Certification Process

1

Scoping & Planning

We begin by working closely with your team to define clear objectives, scope of testing, rules of engagement, and project timelines to ensure alignment.

2

Discovery & Analysis

Our experts utilize a blend of automated scanning and manual reconnaissance techniques to map your attack surface and identify potential vulnerabilities.

3

Exploitation & Testing

In a controlled and safe manner, we simulate real-world attack scenarios to exploit identified weaknesses, confirming their existence and assessing their business impact.

4

Reporting & Analysis

We compile a comprehensive report detailing all findings, including risk ratings, Proof-of-Concepts (PoCs), and clear, actionable remediation guidance.

5

Re-Testing

After your team has implemented the fixes, we conduct a thorough re-test to verify that all identified vulnerabilities have been successfully remediated.

6

Final Report Submission

We deliver the final, certified report that confirms the resolved status of all findings, serving as a formal attestation of your improved security posture.

Key Security Questions You Need to Ask

What are our most critical assets that need protection?

Identify your organization's crown jewels - the data, systems, and applications that would cause the most damage if compromised.

How would we detect a security breach?

Evaluate your monitoring capabilities, incident detection systems, and response procedures to ensure timely identification of security incidents.

What is our incident response plan?

Ensure you have a documented and tested incident response plan that outlines roles, responsibilities, and procedures for handling security incidents.

How often do we conduct security assessments?

Regular security assessments are crucial. Determine the frequency based on your risk profile, compliance requirements, and system changes.

Are our third-party vendors secure?

Evaluate the security practices of your vendors and partners, as their vulnerabilities can become your vulnerabilities.
